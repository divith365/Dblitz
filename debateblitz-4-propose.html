Modi vs Opposition — Budget 2025
Politics Jul 5 · 84 min · 4 debators · 🇮🇳
Full transcript
Key moments
Summary
00:00 Priya M. Welcome to DebateBlitz. Today's debate is on the Union Budget 2025 — growth or burden. We have four distinguished voices. Let us begin with opening statements. Arjun, please go ahead.
00:42 Arjun S. Thank you Priya. The Budget 2025 is the most growth-oriented budget in recent memory. Capital expenditure at 11.1 lakh crore is unprecedented. Infrastructure spending has doubled in three years. This is not a burden — this is an investment in India's future.
02:18 Rahul K. I respectfully disagree. The middle class is being systematically ignored. Income tax relief is cosmetic at best. The fiscal deficit target of 4.9 percent is being achieved on the backs of ordinary taxpayers, not through genuine fiscal prudence.
04:05 Vikram N. Let us look at the numbers objectively. GDP growth at 7.6 percent, inflation contained at 4.2 percent, foreign reserves at an all-time high. These are not accidents — they are the direct result of disciplined fiscal policy that this budget continues.
06:32 Nandita K. But who benefits? The rural employment guarantee scheme has seen a real-term cut. Agricultural subsidies are declining year on year. The wealth gap between urban and rural India is widening, and this budget does nothing meaningful to address that structural problem.
08:44 Priya M. Excellent opening statements. Let us move to rebuttals. Arjun, two minutes to respond to Rahul's point on the middle class.
09:02 Arjun S. Rahul is correct that direct tax relief is modest — but he misses the bigger picture. When capex drives growth, it creates jobs. Jobs create income. Income for the middle class. The PM Awas Yojana expansion alone will touch 2 crore families this year. That is not cosmetic.
11:30 Rahul K. With all due respect — trickle-down economics has a poor track record globally. The evidence is clear: direct transfers and tax cuts for the middle class have a higher consumption multiplier than capex-driven growth. We are choosing the slower path deliberately.
14:10 Vikram N. I want to bring in the global context here. India is one of only three major economies maintaining above 6% growth. The IMF itself revised India's forecast upward after this budget. No other G20 country has managed this combination of growth and fiscal consolidation simultaneously.
16:48 Nandita K. GDP growth numbers are macro statistics that hide enormous micro-level pain. When I speak to families in Tamil Nadu and Bihar, they are not feeling this growth. Onion prices, fuel prices, education costs — the daily lived reality tells a very different story from the finance ministry's press releases.
